01 The Product

A study tool that fits in a commute.

02 The Problem

A certification exam doesn't care that you're also doing an MSc.

The CIPP/E is Europe's benchmark privacy certification — dense with articles, recitals, case law and edge cases — and I'm preparing for it alongside a full MSc and evaluation work. What I didn't have was two-hour textbook sessions. What I did have was fragments: ten minutes on the Luas, five in a queue, twenty before a lecture.

So the tool had to meet the time where it lived: high-frequency micro-study, on the phone, offline, with the app deciding what to review next so I never spend attention on scheduling.

03 What It Does

Spaced repetition, engineered for thumbs.

  • Spaced-repetition drills. Every GDPR concept is a card with a review schedule; the Drill tab shows a "due" dot so the day's queue is one tap away. The algorithm decides what I see — I just answer.
  • Installable PWA. Added to the home screen it behaves like a native app: full-screen, instant, with a proper tab bar (58px tap targets — sized for thumbs, not cursors).
  • Offline-first. A service worker caches the whole app, so it works in a tunnel, on a plane, or with no signal — which is exactly where study minutes hide.
  • Zero-cost pipeline. Built and deployed through GitHub Pages with Actions; push to redeploy, auto-updates on next open.
04 Built With Claude

Iterated like a product, not a side project.

Claude and I built this in tight loops: describe the friction, ship the fix, test it on the actual phone, repeat. The mobile tab bar alone went through several rounds — icons, label sizes, tap-target heights — because a study app that's 5% annoying gets opened 100% less.

The best lesson came from a bug that wasn't a bug: after one update, my phone kept showing the old version. Diagnosis: the service worker's cache, doing its job too well. Working through cache-busting, update flows and deploy-vs-cache diagnostics with Claude taught me more about how PWAs really behave in the field than any tutorial — and it's the kind of failure mode (stale model, stale policy, stale cache) that governance work is full of.

"Encoding the law is the fastest way to learn it — a flashcard you wrote is worth ten you downloaded."

The real outcome is upstream of the exam: writing and drilling every card forced me to argue with the GDPR text itself — which articles interlock, where the edge cases live, what the regulator actually expects. That fluency feeds straight into the AI governance work this whole site is about.

07 What I Learned

Tools get used for small reasons, and abandoned for smaller ones.

Key Takeaways
  • Friction is the real feature flag. Offline support and thumb-sized targets did more for study consistency than any content improvement.
  • Caches are policies. The stale-service-worker episode is the same problem as a stale model card or an outdated DPIA: what's deployed and what's documented drift apart unless you engineer the update path.
  • Teaching a machine the law teaches you the law. Same lesson as my compliance app, confirmed twice now.
